A one-page refresher of the opening bids, responses, and simple competition from
your first six lessons. Count **high-card points** first — A=4, K=3, Q=2, J=1 —
and add length as the auction develops.

## Opening the bidding

```response-box
title: What to open with 13+ points
1NT | 15–17 HCP, balanced (no singleton or void, at most one doubleton)
1♥ / 1♠ | 13–21 points, five or more cards in the major
1♣ / 1♦ | 13–21 points, no five-card major — open your longer minor
Pass | Fewer than 13 points
```

Open the higher of two five-card majors. With 4-4 in the minors open 1♦; with
3-3 open 1♣.

## Responding to a major

```response-box
title: After partner opens 1♥ or 1♠
Raise to 2 | 6–9 points, three or more trumps
Raise to 3 | 10–12 points, three or more trumps (invitational)
1NT | 6–9 points, fewer than three trumps
New suit | 6+ points and forcing — bid it at the one level when you can
3NT | 13–15 balanced, denies three-card support
```

## Responding to 1NT

```response-box
title: After partner opens 1NT (15–17)
Pass | 0–7 points, balanced
2♣ Stayman | 8+ points, asking for a four-card major
2♦ / 2♥ transfer | Five+ hearts (2♦) or spades (2♥); partner bids your suit
3NT | 10–15 points, no four-card major
```

A transfer names the suit *below* your major, so the strong 1NT hand becomes
declarer and its high cards stay hidden.

## When the opponents open

```response-box
title: Competing over their opening
Overcall (1 level) | 8–16 points, a good five-card suit
Overcall 1NT | 15–18 balanced with a stopper in their suit
Takeout double | 13+ points, short in their suit, support for the others
Pass | No good suit and no takeout shape — wait
```

## How high to go

```response-box
title: Combined-point targets
Part score | 20–24 — stop at the two or three level
Game | 25–26 — 3NT, 4♥ / 4♠, or 5♣ / 5♦
Small slam | 33 — bid six
Grand slam | 37 — bid seven
```

## A sample auction

Stayman finds the 4–4 heart fit and the partnership reaches game:

```auction
dealer: S
1NT  P    2C^1 P
2H^2 P    4H   P
---
1. Stayman — asking for a four-card major
2. "Yes, I have four hearts"
```
