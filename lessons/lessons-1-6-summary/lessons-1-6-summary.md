A quick refresher of your first six lessons.

## Counting your points

````row
Count your points the moment you pick up your hand: high-card points plus
length points — one point for every card over four in a suit. This hand has
15 HCP, and its six-card spade suit is worth 2 more, marked on the 5th and 6th
cards.

```response-box
title: High-card points
A | 4
K | 3
Q | 2
J | 1
```
````

## Opening the bidding

```response-box
title: Opening with 13+ points
1NT | 15–17, balanced
1♥ / 1♠ | Five+ cards in the major
1♣ / 1♦ | No five-card major; longer minor
Pass | Fewer than 13 points
```

Higher of two five-card majors; 1♦ with 4-4 minors, 1♣ with 3-3.

## Responding to a major

```response-box
title: After 1♥ or 1♠
Raise to 2 | 6–9, three+ trumps
Raise to 3 | 10–12, three+ trumps
1NT | 6–9, short trumps
New suit | 6+, forcing
```

## Responding to 1NT

```response-box
title: After 1NT (15–17)
Pass | 0–7, balanced
2♣ | Stayman: 8+, asks for a major
2♦ / 2♥ | Transfer to hearts / spades
3NT | 10–15, no four-card major
```

A transfer names the suit below your major, so the 1NT hand declares.

## Competing

```response-box
title: Over their opening
Overcall | 8–16, good five-card suit

Double | 13+, short in their suit
```
